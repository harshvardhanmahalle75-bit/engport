Project Overview

A detailed creep and fatigue assessment was performed for HRSG Unit 3 at Immingham Power Station to evaluate long-term structural integrity. The study simulated real operating conditions, including thermal and pressure loading over steady and cyclic scenarios.

Scope of Work

Reconstructed 3D CAD models from OEM drawings for critical pressure components:

  • HP Superheater 1: 7 headers + 1 manifold
  • HP Superheater 2: 3 headers + 2 manifolds
  • Attemperator: 1 Unit
Methodology

Creep Analysis

Evaluated based on steady-state temperature and internal pressure loading.

Fatigue Analysis

Based on pressure cycles combined with transient thermal effects (start-up conditions).

Key Findings

Creep Hotspots

  • UH1 (HP SH 2): 17.87% projected usage
  • LH3 (HP SH 1): 16.49% projected usage

Fatigue Critical

  • UH1 (HP SH 1): 18.03% projected usage
Recommendations & Value
  • Install instrumentation on key headers to monitor real thermal transients
  • Focus targeted NDT inspections on nozzle connections and branch intersections
  • Established life-limiting components through 2047
  • Supported risk-based maintenance planning

HHP Header Valve Bypass Connection Design

ASME Section I Compliance · VPI Immingham

Redesign of a High High Pressure (HHP) header valve bypass to replace non-compliant socket welds with full-penetration welded connections.

Eliminated socket weld fatigue risk in high-pressure service
Verified ASME Section I compliance across multiple sensitivity cases
Delivered full fabrication drawing for manufacture
ASME Section I Mechanical Design VPI Immingham Pressure Components
▾ View Detailed Report
Replacement Design
Problem Statement

A non-compliant socket weld connection was identified on a newly supplied valve. Socket welds are prohibited in high-pressure steam service at this site due to high fatigue cracking risk and limited inspection capabilities.

Design & Analysis Approach

Three design sensitivity cases were evaluated to validate robustness using ASME Section I requirements:

  • Case 1 (Baseline): Based on real geometry assumptions.
  • Case 2 (Material): P91 equivalent material representation.
  • Case 3 (Geometry): Reduced thickness bounding case.
Key Results & Decisions

Usage Ratios

  • Main Body: ~31% – 39% (Acceptable)
  • Nozzle: ~39% – 68% (Acceptable)

Justification

  • Selected Full Penetration Weld to stop fatigue
  • Forged/Machined single component for reliability
  • P91/F91 material compatibility confirmed
Value Delivered
  • Delivered fully ASME-compliant high-pressure design
  • Improved fatigue resistance and reduced inspection burden
  • Reduced weld count to improve overall reliability
  • Enabled safe installation without altering the valve body
